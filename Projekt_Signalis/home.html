Wake Up

Wake up. Remember what you promised. Your ship has crashed on an unknown planet. Systems are down, beyond repair. As you assess the wreckage, you discover your partner — your pilot — is missing. So is one of the spacesuits. There's only one path left to take.

You leave the ship — Penrose 512, a scout shuttle crafted to carry humanity's emissaries into the dark stretches of alien stars. It was a cheap but dependable vessel. Now, it's ruined. You step out into the unknown, leaving the wreck behind.

The only greeting is the blizzard, howling around you, an endless storm that conceals the landscape. You can barely see two meters ahead. There's no sign of her — Alina Seo, wasn't it? Yes, that was her name.

Questions emerge, drifting towards you. But one of them overshadows all the rest: Who are you? Can you even answer that?

You're not human. You are a Replika unit, codename LSTR-512—a Kosmo-Pioneer specialist android. Your mission was simple: to maintain Penrose during its journey and assist your Gestalt pilot. But that mission is meaningless now. You push through the blizzard, until you find... a hole, burrowed into the frozen ground. An unsettling sight, for it's not just a hole—it's lined with red, raw flesh.

You descend, moving deeper into the unknown. The tunnel leads you to... a room. Strangely welcoming, almost cozy. An old radio rests on the table beside a worn, ancient-looking book — The King in Yellow. You pick it up, and the radio crackles to life, broadcasting garbled numbers in a distorted voice. Your vision wavers, and then you hear it — her voice, familiar yet haunting, whispering:

Great holes secretly are digged where earths pores ought to suffice and things have learnt to walk that ought to crawl.

Actual introduction

SIGNALIS is a survival horror video game developed by Rose-Engine and published by Humble Games and Playism, released on October 27, 2022. The game features an unusual aesthetic that merges retrofuturism with anime, immersing players in a dystopian world ruled by a totalitarian regime and plagued by endless war.

The gameplay draws clear inspiration from Resident Evil, combining exploration and resource management with old-fashioned puzzles and top-down shooting. Set in a distant, unspecified solar system (with fan theories suggesting it may be a far-future version of our own, unrecognizable due to passage of time).

The story follows Elster, a Replika unit, whose ship has crash-landed on an unknown planet. Elster awakens to find her partner missing, and after a mysterious prologue described above, she regains consciousness in a bathroom within S-23 Sierpinski — a production and mining facility on the planet... Leng, a part of her home solar system. She states her goal is to find Alina Seo.

Facility seems to be abondoned at first but when Elster ventures deeper she finds dead replika units, both regular workers and combat types. It doesn't take too much time for one of the corpses to come to live screaming at her and then you realize that Resident Evil inspirations went deeper than puzzles and inventory management.

Further exploration reveals that most of the Gestalt (in-game term for humans) staff are dead, and the Replika workforce has degenerated into mindless, hostile beings. Gathering supplies and clues, Elster moves through the eerie facility, determined to uncover Alina's fate.

By the end of the first part Elster finds Replika which seems to function normally - ADLR class, designation Adler - facility's administrator. Things finally appear to take a turn for the better when Elster shows Alina's photo to Adler. His expression suddenly becomes gloomy when he looks back at her and pushes her down the elevator shaft saying.

You shouldn't have returned

Well... is seems there is a lot more to learn.

Expectations

SIGNALIS is a game that requires a specific mindset to fully appreciate. One of the biggest mistakes you can make is assuming the story will be straightforward and fully comprehensible after a single playthrough. I made this assumption myself—seeing the game's taking inspiration from Resident Evil, I expected a similarly direct narrative.

Unfortunately, SIGNALIS doesn't offer this kind of experience. The plot here is convoluted, often contradictory and... well, let's just say that the events you experience aren't exactly real. Just like in a dream. You might have realized that after reading about beginning of the game. It throws a lot of mysteries and information at you and it doesn't give clear answer to practically any of it.

Did you notice, in the previous section of this article, that the photo of Elster's copilot and Alina Seo, who main character says is her copilot, are... different? They don't look alike. The game doesn't tell you why Elster seems to ignore that fact and randomly starts looking for a different woman until the end of the game! And by tell you I mean that it gives you enough information so that you can piece some answers together on your own.

This sort of pattern repeats practically in every plot point in the game. When I first finished it I had no idea what actually happened, what was supposed to be real and what was just a dream?. Was it all a dream? Moreover, I was very frustrated since I considered games that leave you in this state at the end to be... well... bad.

But I didn't stop there. I began to dig deeper—replaying parts of the game, reading others' theories, and reflecting on my experiences. Gradually, a bigger picture started to emerge. As it turns our, SIGNALIS actually does provide answers to nearly every essential question! They're just hidden and require more than a single playthrough to uncover and as someone who discovered them I can assure you - it is worth it.
