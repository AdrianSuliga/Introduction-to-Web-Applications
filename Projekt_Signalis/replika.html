What are Replikas?

A Replika is a biomechanical, synthetic, and neural reproduction of a person. Created by copying the neural pattern of a human to form a new Replika Unit, their memories are suppressed and their skills kept intact to preserve the Replikas predictability and productivity. Humans chosen to serve as a neural pattern are permanently cryogenically preserved and erased from public memory to serve Eusan Nation as an incorruptible ideal.

Manufactured like this Replikas will not be able to recall anything from its human life under normal circumstances. However, even the best-maintained Replika unit will eventually be exposed to stimuli that may cause human memories to resurface. While units degraded in such way pose no particular danger, like all units with Persona degradation, they will eventually lose many of their advantages over regular workers.

Persona degradation is usually difficult to identify, so they are kept under watch by commanding units who monitor their behaviour. It is recommended to dispose of a unit as soon as it shows any signs of individualism.

Replikas can be classified by their unit class which describes their frame, neural patterns and optimal roles. They also receive more casual designation names all of which are corresponding to a bird name in german.

EULR

Unit class EULR (Einfache-Universelle-Leichte Replika), (Simple Universal Light Replika), designation name Eule are low-cost general purpose Replikas used for lighter jobs such as cooking, nursing, cleaning and simple office job.

EULR units are the backbone of the Eusan Nation's workforce. These elegant multi-purpose worker units are highly social and get along well with each other and most other Replika models. While unfit for combat, their lightweight frame makes them a prime choice for distant facilities where supplies are limited.

Eules tend to form large groups and like to sing and dance, as the original neural pattern for this unit was a ballet dancer. Persona degradation can be easily prevented in this unit by making sure they have access to music (through tape players or musical instruments). Always place at least one mirror in Eule dorms, as they have a strong urge to check their own appearance at regular intervals. Eules' Persona stabilizes by keeping a tidy appearance and through regular social interactions. Eules will often organize in groups of roughly ten units and give each other themed nicknames.

Degraded Eules serve as standard oponent during the game. They usually carry knives and other daily used items which they most likely obtained from their initial roles. They also tend to gather in groups or accompany other degraded units.

ARAR

Unit class ARAR (Allzweck-Reparatur-Arbeiter Replika), (All-Purpose Repair Worker Replika), designation name Ara are low-cost general purpose Replikas used in repair, maintenance, construction and production of industrial goods.

One of the earliest Replika designs, the simple but efficient Aras are actually the most-produced Replika type. They've replaced all human workers in especially dangerous working conditions. Aras often construct service tunnels accessible only to them, usually under floors and in walls. Unstable units may retreat into these tunnels. It is not recommended to attempt to retrieve them.

Even though they may seem like quiet simpletons, they are not to be underestimated. While they may not show it, they judge those who are rude or unkind harshly and will quietly share this judgment with their entire Cadre. Aras get along best with Eules, who tend to be patient and friendly, and have a talent for reading Aras' expressionless faces. Their Personalities can be stabilized by allowing them access to plants to take care of, ideally colorful flowers or trees.

Corrupted Aras have inflated puffy arms and a corroded head, are hunched over, exposing the skin on their back which has degraded to the point of exposing flesh and their spinal structure underneath. They hide in the vents of the facility before emerging from underneath the floor to attack the player.

LSTR

Unit class LSTR (Landvermessungs-Schiff-Techniker Replika), (Land Survey, Ship Technician Replika), designation name Elster are Kosmo-Pioneer Specialist designed to assist orbital missions and scout harsh terrain.

LSTRs have a Titanium Skeleton Frame, which contains a Repair Logic Module. This Module can be used by LSTR units to easily repair themselves and crucial mission equipment. Elsters' technical knowledge and combat capabilities make these units true survivalists

Stoic and reserved, they have a relatively stable neural pattern. It is best for human partners to leave it alone and interact with the Elster unit as little as possible. Elsters' neural pattern was a soldier of Vinetan origin, so their needs are basic. Avoid talking to the Elster unit about the war. To avoid resurfacing of human memories, do not show or give the Elster unit photographs, especially of soldiers during the war. Do not show the Elster unit movies, or let it listen to music. Do not try to befriend the Elster unit.

The original neural pattern for this unit was lost with the destruction of the central Neural Archive on Vineta, new LSTR units have been produced based on a decommissioned unit from the Penrose Program.

ADLR

Unit class ADLR (Administration, Datenverarbeitung, Logistik-Replika), (Administration, Data Processing, Logistics Replika), designation name Adler are command specialist Replika. They are employed as facility administrators across Eusan Nation, often serving as adjutant to FKLR units.

An integral part of every facility is the Administrator unit. A single ADLR unit can manage and oversee all administrative tasks for an entire facility, freeing other Operation Command units to focus on the direct control of Protektor units.

Adler units work best when left alone. They do not mix well with their own type. They will usually become very emotionally dependent on their Commander. Adlers are very susceptible to bioresonant suggestion, making them fairly easy to control. However, they become bored very easily and may need new Fetish objects at regular intervals for stabilization.

Adler which players meet in a game serves as the antagonist for the story. We learn that his commanding FKLR unit fell ill due to an unknown sickness carried from the mines which led him to a lot of emotional stress and Persona destabilization. Said sickness quickly spread on other Replika units avoiding ADLR for an unknown reason.

MNHR

Unit class MNHR (Minenarbeit, Nukleartechnik, Hochsicherheits-Replika), (Mining, Nuclear Tech, High-Security Replika), designation name Mynah are industrial specialist Replika. They are deployed in hazardous work environments across Eusan Nation such as mines or nuclear power plants.

When it comes to dangerous cargo, heavy machinery and hazardous environments, no other model rivals the MNHR units with their high-security power armor bodies. Even in lethal radiation, under crushing pressure and in zero G, they keep their calm demeanor and show exemplary teamwork. Despite their hulking bodies, underneath their face shields a standard Generation 3 Replika face can be found, making maintenance and social interfacing as easy as with any other Replika Model. Please note that the MNHR neural pattern is not suited for combat use.

Their neural pattern is extremely stable. Little has to be done to keep Mynahs happy. They get along well with most other models thanks to their sometimes submissive, sometimes motherly personality. Unlike other models, diary-keeping is not mandatory for Persona stabilization. Small stuffed animals may be given as fetish objects, though those resembling cats should be avoided as they might cause resurfacing Gestalt memories.

KLBR

Unit class KLBR (Kommando-Leiteinheit Bioresonanztechnik-Replika), (Commando Control Unit Bioresonance Technology Replika), designation name Kolibri are bioresonance spiecialist Replika. These units serve as a political officer, utilizing their resonators to manipulate the minds of both Replika and humans alike.

They are a Marvel of modern technology, the Kolibri is the most capable bioresonance unit ever produced. Every FKLR unit is aided by a cadre of KLBR unit adjutants, which can amplify her bioresonant signals. Despite their diminutive build, Kolibris are one of the most effective Protektor units as they are able to directly influence the minds of Replikas and humans.

Great care should be given to Kolibris as their neural patterns are very unstable. Like most bioresonant individuals, Kolibris will often subconsciously create an emotional feedback loop, imitating and then broadcasting the emotions of those around them. That's why already unstable units can sometimes spiral into Persona degradation. The constant exchange of memories and emotions between units of a Cadre acts as a safety net that buffers extreme changes. However, once a majority of units in a Cadre degrade, they will drag the remaining units down with them. For Persona stabilization, Kolibris should have access to a well-stocked library.

STAR

Unit class STAR (Sicherheitstechniker Aufseher Replika), (Security Technician Guard Replika), designation name Star are low-cost combat units. They serve as protektor security and technician guards

The Standard model of the Protektor Security Technicians. Fitted with extended legs, STAR units gracefully tower over most humans. Despite their heavy armor, they can move swiftly with their long stride. Their cool and detached demeanor allows them to analyze situations with objectivity and deploy force as required. Trained in close combat and riot control techniques, they operate best in small squads led by an officer STAR unit equipped with a ballistic shield.

Despite their normally laid-back demeanor, STARs have a strong internal hierarchy, which is important to take into consideration when promoting units to officers. Not promoting a respected unit or promoting a unit low in status can lead to friction within dorms. STARs will occasionally develop in-group rules involving physical punishments. It is recommended to allow some Officers to own military weapons as Fetish objects to stabilize their Persona.

STARs are one of the most irritating enemies during the game since they carry a ballistic shield with them. They are also often accompanied by weaker units.

STCR

Unit class STCR (Sicherheitstechniker Controller Replika), (Security Technician Controller Replika), designation name Storch are combat lead replikas. Storches are deployed to oversee and coordinate STARs.

Storch units initially have a short temper. Training them in patience early after deployment is key, as their neural patterns are less stable then other models. Failing to do so may yield an extremely volatile personality prone to cruelty and violence. A common strategy is pairing them closely with an older Star unit. Storch's Persona stabilizes by showering or bathing. Books on history or mythology work well as Fetish objects.

Each Cadre of STARs is overseen by a Controller Unit STCR. One of the tallest Replika models thanks to their extended legs, Storks figuratively and literally keep a constant birds-eye view of any situation ready to direct and coordinate their assigned Security Technicians. Their tough, no-nonsense demeanor, though sometimes described as brutal and even cruel, makes them the perfect fit as unwavering sentinels of order.

They serve as toughest regural enemies, usually accompanied by weaker units they pose a significant threat to the player. After degradation they were distorted almost beyond recognition, with their arms missing and their heads replaced with a strange beak-like growth.
